Baseline Assessment

Before your personalised learning journey begins, we need to understand your current digital skill levels. This assessment takes around 12 minutes and runs once - your results are used immediately to tailor every learning nudge you receive.

About this assessment

  • 21 scored questions across 7 skill categories
  • Plus 5 optional personal development questions at the end
  • Each category has 2 self-report questions and 1 knowledge check
  • Approximately 15 minutes to complete (core: ~12 min + optional PD: ~3 min)
  • Runs once on first login - never repeated
  • Answer options are not in any obvious order - read each one carefully
  • Your results are passed directly to the Observer agent
DigComp Data Literacy Generative AI Automation Power BI M365 Cybersecurity

How it was created

  • Based on Repsol's Target Skills Matrix and the EU DigComp 2.2 framework
  • Categories aligned to Repsol's internal course curriculum (Data & AI, Power BI, Copilot/M365, and more)
  • Hybrid format prevents self-report inflation - knowledge checks verify applied understanding
  • Output feeds into the Observer agent to personalise your learning nudges from day one
  • Designed as the Evaluator agent's baseline - growth is measured against this starting point

Scoring system

Level Label What it means
1 Awareness I know the concept exists but have not applied it in practice
2 Basic I have used it for simple tasks, sometimes with guidance
3 Intermediate I use it confidently and independently in my day-to-day work
4 Expert I can teach others and handle complex, advanced scenarios
Self-Report questions - Each option carries a hidden score from 1 to 4 based on the level of skill or experience it describes. Answer options are deliberately not presented in order of proficiency - the system scores your answer based on what it describes, not its position in the list.

Knowledge Check questions - One objectively correct answer exists. Correct = 3 points. Wrong = 1 point. This is intentional: getting it right confirms at least intermediate applied knowledge; getting it wrong flags a gap even if you self-reported high confidence.

Category score - Average of all 3 question scores in the category, rounded down to the nearest integer (floor). Range: 1–4.
Overconfidence cap - If you answer the knowledge check incorrectly and your self-report average is 3 or above, your category score is capped at 2. This prevents the system placing you at Intermediate or Expert level based solely on self-assessment when your applied knowledge does not match. The Skill Matcher agent receives this as a knowledge_gap flag and prioritises a foundational nudge for that category before any other content.

Output flags

immediate_nudge - Final category score = 1
knowledge_gap - KC wrong + self-report avg ≥ 3

What happens next?

Your skill profile is passed to the Observer agent, which reads your results alongside your digital context (calendar, tasks, activity) to identify skill gaps and prioritise which ones matter most right now.

The Content Curator then retrieves the most relevant learning content - internal Repsol courses first, then external sources - matched to those gaps. The Formatter delivers that content to you in the right format for your current context (a quick nudge, an audio clip, or a full course). Finally, the Evaluator creates a personalised assessment based on what you have learned to measure your actual level. The process ends there - your growth is recorded and available in your profile.
